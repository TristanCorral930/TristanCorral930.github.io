Computer Controlled Cutting

This page is dedicated to my Computer Controlled Cutting project. Here, I detail the process, tools, designs, and outcomes of this fabrication technique.

Project Overview

Computer Controlled Cutting involves the use of laser cutters and vinyl cutters to precisely shape materials. In this project, I experimented with designing vector files and producing components for a larger assembly.

Project Media

Demonstration Video

Reflection

Through this project, I learned about tolerances in laser cutting, vector preparation, and material limitations. The cyberpunk aesthetic was achieved by integrating glowing acrylic elements with layered cuts.

Vinyl Sticker Documentation

Design

I created the sticker design using the Affinity Software. The main challenge was simplifying the shapes so that the cutter could process each of them clearly.

Cutting

I sorted the colors from the vector path to make sure every part of the logo would be accounted for. I also monitored the machine to ensure no mistakes would be made.

Weeding

I weeded the excess vinyl around the design's prints. The entire process was delicate, especially cutting around the thin lines around each of the letters and the explosion component.

Applying

I applied transfer tape and carefully placed the entire sticker onto the designated surface. I Pressed down firmly to ensure a smooth, complete final product.

Mini-Reflection

The hardest part of the vinyl cutting process was weeding the fine details without tearing the design up. I solved this problem by using a weeding tool and working on it slowly. Overall, the process went smoothly even though our final vinyl design included a more than usual amount of colors.

Laser Cutting

Laser cutting allowed for precise cuts on materials like wood and acrylic. Documentation for this step will be expanded later with images and notes.
